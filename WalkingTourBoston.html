Self-Guided Walking Tour of Literary Boston

The city and surrounding districts of Boston, Massachusetts possess a renown American literary history that beckons the curious soul. The intriguing landmarks and artifacts of celebrated women and men of letters are yours to discover. Don your walking shoes and together we'll explore!

North End

Beautiful Italian shops and restaurants proudly stand among the former residences of minister and prolific orator Cotton Mather and poet John Ciardi in Boston's North End district.

Let's go! »

Downtown Boston

Boston city central is a treasure trove of literary beacons encompassing everything from the birthplaces of Benjamin Franklin and Edgar Allen Poe to Elizabeth Peabody's Book Shop.

Let's go! »

Beacon Hill

With the brilliant golden dome of the Massachusetts State House gleaming above a well-manicured public garden, Beacon Hill experienced amazing historical acvities that inspired Robert Lowell's work.

Let's go! »

Copley Square

Copley's star, the Boston Public Library, founded in 1848, is one of America's largest municipal public library systems. It houses over 23.7 million items.

Let's go! »

Fenway

Fenway Park is not only beloved to sports fans worldwide, but a critical element in American literary and journalistic cultures.

Let's go! »
